Social Cognitive Neuroscience Laboratory

In this experiment, we are interested in how people make judgments about
other people's emotion reactions.

This HIT consists of 9 trials in which you will read short descriptions of events and judge what emotions you think the people in those events would experience. This is followed by 8 emotion matching questions.


Note: • This HIT requires that you are fluent (expert) in English.
• You will need to be able to play audio in order to successfully complete this HIT.
• This HIT requires that you have a moderately fast internet connection (in order to load the video/audio media).

Disclaimer: This HIT is part of a scientific research project. Your decision to complete this HIT is voluntary. There is no way for us to identify you. The only information we will have, in addition to your responses, is the time at which you completed the survey. The results of the research may be presented at scientific meetings or published in scientific journals. Instructions 1 of 5:

In this experiment, you will see 9 brief descriptions of events and judge the emotions of the people involved. These are followed by 8 short matching questions.

Please note that this HIT has a rather long training. We ask that you go slow and think carefully about your answers. We expect it to take about 15-20 minutes to complete.

---------------------------------

All of the people you will consider are contestants in the final round of a game show.

Over the course of the show, two contestants have interacted in games involving strategic deception and honesty to add money to a total jackpot.

In the final round, all of the money that the two contestants have accumulated is at stake. These jackpots can range from $1.00 to over $100,000.00.


Each contestant is given a choice: whether to 'Split' or 'Steal'.

If both players choose to 'Split', they each win half of the total jackpot.

If one player chooses to 'Steal' while the other player chooses to 'Split', the player who Stole wins the entire jackpot while the player who Split wins nothing.

If both players choose to 'Steal', then they both leave having won nothing.

Instructions 2 of 5:



You will be presented with information about the players and their decisions, and then asked to rate the extent to which a particular player experienced 20 different emotions.

Prior to revealing their decisions to one another, the contestants negotiate with each other in the hopes of convincing the other person to choose 'Split', usually by asserting that they themselves will also 'Split'.

In order to convince the other player to 'Split', players tend to place their reputation on the line, make bold appeals to their honor and morals, and/or describe the extreme consequences that they would suffer if they were to 'Steal'. ("I promised my kids I would Split and they would never look up to me again if they saw me Steal", "I would definitely get fired from my job if I Stole", etc.)

After negotiating, the contestants simultaneously reveal their decisions by showing each other the word 'Split' or 'Steal' written inside of a gold-colored ball.

(Again, for one player to win anything, the other player much choose 'Split', but there is no financial incentive for either player to 'Split'.)


The entire event takes place in front of a live studio audience, is broadcast on TV nationally, and is widely viewed. Contestants on the show are often recognized months or years later by strangers who remember the contestant's on-air behavior.

Instructions 5 of 5:




For each person you consider, you will be told the total size of the jackpot, the decision they made, what the other player chose, and how much each player won as a result. You will also be shown a photo of the contestant that you are providing emotion ratings for.

The photo was taken in the seconds immediately leading up to the contestants revealing their decisions. The photo you are shown is NOT of the person's reaction to the decision. You should therefore not judge the person's emotions at the time of the photo, but rather judge what emotions you expect the person would have felt directly after learning the outcome of the 'Split'/'Steal' game.



If you think that the person is definitely not experiencing a particular emotion, move the sliding marker all the way to the left to indicate "not any". (Whereas, if you do think that the person is experiencing the emotion, even slightly or subtly, move the marker to the right accordingly.)
