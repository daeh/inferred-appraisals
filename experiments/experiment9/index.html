Social Cognitive Neuroscience Laboratory

In this experiment, we are interested in how people make judgments about
other people's motivations and values.

This HIT consists of 9 trials in which you will read short descriptions of events and assess what motivations/values you think caused people to act the ways that they did.

Instructions 1 of 6:

In this experiment, you will read 9 short event descriptions and then judge the motivations of a person in the event. These are followed by 4 matching questions.

Please note that this HIT has a rather long training. We ask that you go slow and think carefully about your answers. We expect it to take about 15-20 minutes to complete.

All of the people you will consider are contestants in the final round of a game show.

Over the course of the show, two contestants have interacted in games involving strategic deception and honesty to add money to a total jackpot.

In the final round, all of the money that the two contestants have accumulated is at stake. These jackpots can range from $1.00 to over $100,000.00.


Each contestant is given a choice: whether to 'Split' or 'Steal'.

If both players choose to 'Split', they each win half of the total jackpot.

If one player chooses to 'Steal' while the other player chooses to 'Split', the player who Stole wins the entire jackpot while the player who Split wins nothing.

If both players choose to 'Steal', then they both leave having won nothing.

Instructions 2 of 6:



In each trial, you will be presented with information about a player's decision and asked to rate how much the player cares about different values.

Prior to revealing their decisions to one another, the contestants negotiate with each other in the hopes of convincing the other person to choose 'Split', usually by asserting that they themselves will also 'Split'.

In order to convince the other player to 'Split', players tend to place their reputation on the line, make bold appeals to their honor and morals, and/or describe the extreme consequences that they would suffer if they were to 'Steal'. ("I promised my kids I would Split and they would never look up to me again if they saw me Steal", "I would definitely get fired from my job if I Stole", etc.)

After negotiating, the contestants simultaneously reveal their decisions by showing each other the word 'Split' or 'Steal' written inside of a gold-colored ball.

(Again, for one player to win anything, the other player much choose 'Split', but there is no financial incentive for either player to 'Split'.)


The entire event takes place in front of a live studio audience, is broadcast on TV nationally, and is widely viewed. Contestants on the show are often recognized months or years later by strangers who remember the contestant's on-air behavior.


Next you'll hear the rules of the game described and watch an example of two contestants negotiating before revealing their 'Split' or 'Steal' decision.

This is the only time audio will be required for this HIT.
